Thesis

In 1955 the Soviet Union and the United States were competing in a ballistic missile-based nuclear arms race. Eventually, this morphed into a competition to achieve superior spaceflight capability. Many citizens of the U.S. didn’t see the point in this race and thought their money could be allocated to things a little closer to home.

In 1969 July 15, a day before the launch of Apollo 11, a group of civil rights activists protested the moon landing. They argued that the money could be spent helping people in poverty by providing food and shelter. Because of this protest, NASA developed technologies to make better housing using the insulation from spaceships, but it was more of an advertising stunt and didn’t amount to much. This protest did still raise awareness, which eventually led to more government spending on helping people in need, and the end of the Apollo missions. Without this protest, America's many social issues might of gone unnoticed.

One day before the launch of Apollo 11, Ralph David Abernathy Sr, a civil rights activist and Baptist minister, led 25 poor families to the Kennedy Space Center to protest the launch of Apollo 11. Calling out America’s distorted sense of priorities, the families brought wagons drawn by mules. The mules’ contrast between the gleaming Saturn V rocket represented the difference between the billions spent on the space program and the poor families who couldn’t afford bare necessities.

This graph shows the government spending on NASA compared to the federal budget. A large spike shows around the 60's, when the space race was in full swing.

The protesters thought that the amount of spending on the space race was unjustified. Billions of dollars were spent on the competion to develop missles and other war technologies, while people were starving on the steet. What's the point in competing in a competion with another nation, when our own nation is falling apart?

The Government thought that the money was well spent, developing important technologies, and showing the Soviet Union they wouldn't back down from a challenge. If we don't develop missles and satelites, the Soviet Union could attack us and we wouldn't be prepared. This is a race to protect our country and our beliefs.

Diplomacy

To combat the peacful protesters, NASA sent out an administrator named Thomas Paine. Abernathy talked to Paine about why the moon landing was negativley affecting the country. He told Paine that he had three requests from NASA, that ten familes from his group could veiw the launch, NASA should support the movemnt to combat this nations poverty, hunger, and other social problems, and that NASA engineers develop technologies to help combat the problem of hunger in America.

Paine replied saying, "If we could solve the problems of poverty in the United States by not pushing the button to launch men to the moon tomorrow, then we would not push that button." Paine also told Abernanthy that he wished he would “hitch his wagons to our rocket, using the space program as a spur to the nation to tackle problems boldly in other areas, and using NASA’s space successes as a yardstick by which progress in other areas should be measured.” The protest then ended with the two men in agreeance, Paine gave Abernanthy 10 VIP veiwing tickets, and promised him they would keep good on their promises. Abernanthy then replied saying he hoped for the astronauts saftey and that he was as proud as anyone for the great accomplisment.

Effects

Despite all of the protests, NASA still continued with the moonshot. On July 16, 1969, NASA launched the shuttle carrying Neil Armstrong, Buzz Aldrin, and Michael Collins. Eight days later on July 24, they landed on the moon, which some argue ended the space race. Although another argument could be made that the USSR won because of the very little spending compared to NASA’s spending and the clear advantage they had. The entire point of the space race was to see who could be the first to develop satellites and ballistic missiles so they could prove their economy was better.

Trying to keep good on their promises, NASA worked on developing technology that would benefit the poor. The engineers took sensors originally used to detect contaminants in space, and modified them to analyze urban air pollution. The other technology they made was using the insulation from space crafts to build new kinds of walls for windows for housing. These didn't amount to much.
